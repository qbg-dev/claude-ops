# PhD Personal Statement Rules

Rules specific to PhD personal statements—the "tell us about yourself" essay that complements the research statement.

**Prerequisites**: Read `SKILL.md` universal rules first.

---

## Rule PS1: Distinct from Research Statement

**Description**: Personal statement must add new information, not repeat the research statement.

**Elaboration**: If both essays mention the same experience (e.g., IOI), use different angles. Research statement: intellectual curiosity angle. Personal statement: perseverance/community angle. Read both essays together and ask: "Does each add something new?"

**Example**:
- ❌ Research statement: "Winning Gold at IOI showed me I wanted to do research." Personal statement: "My IOI journey taught me about algorithms."
- ✅ Research statement: "The path to Gold revealed my interest in understanding why algorithms work." Personal statement: "After winning Gold, I became what I had lacked—a coach ensuring future students wouldn't face the same isolation I did."

---

## Rule PS2: Meaning-Beyond-Self Throughline

**Description**: Show that your work has purpose beyond personal achievement.

**Elaboration**: "I thrive when what I do has meaning beyond myself" is a strong throughline for personal statements. Every experience should demonstrate this: coaching wasn't just teaching—it was ensuring others wouldn't face your barriers. Rebuilding the team wasn't just playing—it was giving the community a team.

**Example**:
- ❌ "I coached the Swiss IOI team and served as scientific director."
- ✅ "Coaching wasn't just about teaching algorithms—it was about ensuring the next generation wouldn't face the isolation I did. Seeing students I mentored reach IOI proved that what I do can have meaning beyond my own achievements."

---

## Rule PS3: Community Building Evidence

**Description**: Provide concrete evidence of building communities.

**Elaboration**: Claims about community require specifics: what was the state before, what did you do, what was the result? "I strengthened communities" is empty. "When I became captain, the team had lost most of its players to graduation; over two years, I rebuilt it from scratch" is concrete.

**Example**:
- ❌ "I've always sought opportunities to strengthen the communities around me."
- ✅ "When I became captain of the Cambridge Handball Club, the team had just lost most of its players and committee to graduation. Over two years, I rebuilt it: recruiting players, designing sessions for all experience levels, and leading us to the Varsity match against Oxford."

---

## Rule PS4: International Perspective Specificity

**Description**: Make claims about international exposure concrete.

**Elaboration**: "I've formed friendships with people from all over the world" is vague. Name the countries, the topic discussed, and the insight gained. "Discussing [topic] with friends from [countries] showed me [specific insight]."

**Example**:
- ❌ "Cambridge immersed me in a truly international community where I've learned that diverse perspectives lead to better solutions."
- ✅ "Discussing national service policy with friends from Singapore, Israel, and Switzerland showed me how differently societies balance individual freedom and collective obligation—a tension I now see in debates about research funding priorities."

---

## Rule PS5: Why PhD, Not Just Why Research

**Description**: Explain why you want a PhD specifically, not just why you like research.

**Elaboration**: The personal statement should address why you've chosen the PhD path—years of structured training, building expertise, contributing to human knowledge—rather than just expressing love of research. Connect your personal journey to the specific nature of doctoral work.

**Example**:
- ❌ "I love research because it lets me explore interesting questions."
- ✅ "Research, for me, is not just about my own understanding, but about expanding what humanity knows. The PhD represents the training I need to make contributions that others can build on."

---

## Rule PS6: Leadership as Empowerment

**Description**: Frame leadership as empowering others, not commanding them.

**Elaboration**: Don't list what you organized or directed. Show how you helped others grow or removed barriers for them. "Building something new requires both vision and attention to where each individual is."

**Example**:
- ❌ "As captain, I organized training sessions, scheduled matches, and led team meetings."
- ✅ "As co-coach, I designed sessions tailored to players at all levels—helping beginners learn fundamentals while challenging experienced players. Building a team requires knowing where each person is."

---

## Rule PS7: Challenges Overcome Without Victimhood

**Description**: Present challenges as obstacles you overcame, not as claims for sympathy.

**Elaboration**: "I faced hardship" invites skepticism. "Rather than accepting this limitation, I..." shows agency. The challenge should demonstrate a capability you developed, not explain why you deserve accommodation.

**Example**:
- ❌ "Switzerland lacked the coaching infrastructure that other countries have, which made my journey very difficult."
- ✅ "Switzerland lacked coaches who could guide students beyond Bronze. Rather than accepting this, I spent two years teaching myself through online resources—progressing through Silver to Gold in 2021."

---

## Rule PS8: No Redundancy with Research Statement

**Description**: Actively audit for repetition between the two essays.

**Elaboration**: Read both essays back-to-back. Any paragraph that could appear in either essay needs revision. The personal statement reveals character and values; the research statement reveals intellectual trajectory. They should feel like different windows into the same person.

**Example**:
- ❌ Both essays describe the summer research project in detail
- ✅ Research statement: details the technical work and what you learned about research methodology. Personal statement: mentions research only to note that the experience confirmed your commitment to academic life.

---

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] PS1: Personal statement adds new info, not redundant with research?
- [ ] PS2: Clear throughline about meaning beyond self?
- [ ] PS3: Community building claims have concrete evidence?
- [ ] PS4: International experience includes specific topics and insights?
- [ ] PS5: Explains why PhD path specifically, not just "love research"?
- [ ] PS6: Leadership framed as empowering others?
- [ ] PS7: Challenges show agency, not victimhood?
- [ ] PS8: No repetition between essays?
