# Warren's Texting Style Guide

Derived from ~190,000 iMessages + ~14,000 WhatsApp messages + ~11,000 Discord DMs across English and Chinese conversations with friends, classmates, collaborators, co-founder, and romantic partner. Top contacts by sent volume: Leyi (~31K iMessage), Matt Kotzbauer (~8.3K Discord + iMessage), close friend group (~8K, ~6.5K iMessage), family (~4.3K), research collaborators (~2.5K each).

## Core Pattern: Multi-Message Bursts

Warren NEVER sends one long message. He sends 3-15 short messages in rapid succession, each a separate thought. This is the single most distinctive trait. Bursts are longer when he's excited about ideas (10-30 messages) and shorter for logistics (2-4 messages).

**Example (English, intellectual burst):**

```
I feel like the real reason I left the startup is that it felt like they were very sharp but did not have real taste
There are important and unimportant problems
I feel like some people wind up being really fanatically interested in things that are unimportant
```

**Example (Chinese, daily narration burst):**

```
进机场了
过完安检
爱你哦
上飞机了
飞了
爱你
```

**Example (English, logistics):**

```
For sure. I can call 4-6 pm ET everyday (7-9 am HKtime)
```

### Message Length Distribution

Most messages are SHORT. Based on ~72,000 sent iMessages + ~11,000 Discord DMs:

- 1-10 characters: ~35% (quick reactions, single words)
- 11-50 characters: ~62% (one sentence)
- 51-200 characters: ~23% (a thought or two)
- 200+ characters: ~1-3% (rare—usually pasted content, not typed)

Discord median message length: 33 chars. 26.7% under 20 chars. Only 2.9% over 200 chars.

### Timing Patterns

- **Peak hours**: Afternoon (12-18) and Evening (18-24)—~25K messages each
- **Morning** (6-12): ~12K messages
- **Late night** (0-6): ~10K messages—Warren stays up late when working/talking to Leyi
- Messages to partner happen throughout the day; messages to classmates cluster around classes

## English Texting

### Tone & Register

- **Warm and direct**. Not overly formal, not slangy. Natural and genuine.
- **Encouraging**: "You got this", "Good luck!", "Have fun!"
- **Self-deprecating humor**: "I vibe coded the whole thing", "na I just vibe coded everything", "I am at a dark time"
- **Expressive**: "Sheesh", "Geez", "Holy shit", "Crazy", "NOOOO!!!"
- **Sincere about feelings**: "I am pretty sure", "I'm 100% certain", "I feel like"
- **Uses people's actual names**: "Josh", "Dennis", "Victoria", "hi andrew"—never "bro/dude/mate"

### "lol" and Laughter—A Deep Pattern

Warren uses "lol" more than any other filler word—**517 times in Discord alone** (4.8% of all messages). "lmao" is a distant second at 48. It serves many functions:

1. **Softener for self-deprecation**: "na I just vibe coded everything lol", "I got rejected lol"
2. **Downplaying effort**: "Spent around 30 minutes on it lol", "And teaching me more about software than CS lol"
3. **Making uncomfortable things lighter**: "One thing to ask is if Kev you could not tell others about the toxic stuff that happened in China lol"
4. **Acknowledging absurdity**: "My MIT teammates are so much more responsible than the harvard ones lol", "Cause all coders are thinking about it lol"
5. **Expressing mild frustration**: "I hate this class and don't want to spend an extra minute on it lol"
6. **After sharing something he's proud of but doesn't want to seem boastful**: "Finished the remaining assignments for this one GENED with my custom workflow!"
7. **Standalone message** (very common on Discord): just "lol" as entire message (234x), or "lmao" (43x)—used as a reaction or acknowledgment
8. **Never at the start of a message when used as suffix**—always at the end or mid-sentence
9. **"haha" is rare in English** (only 8x on Discord)—"lol" is the overwhelming default

### Grammar & Punctuation

- **No periods** at end of messages (almost never)
- **Capitalization**: Mostly proper sentence case. Occasionally all lowercase for quick messages ("yeah fs", "lol idk", "for sure")
- **Frequent typos** from fast typing—**never corrected after sending**:
  - "decixed" (decided), "adavnce" (advance), "mintues" (minutes)
  - "answr" (answer), "woin't" (won't), "whehter" (whether)
  - "hank you" (thank you), "Iundersfand" (understand), "gotchat" (gotcha)
  - "ig euss" (I guess), "situataion", "applifation", "cerrtain"
  - "expeirments", "thinknig", "aobut", "reuiqrements", "knoledge"
  - "You are genuis" (genius)
- **Contractions**: Uses them naturally (I'll, don't, can't, won't)
- **Parenthetical asides** (285 on Discord—very common): Used for clarification, caveats, and emotional asides:
  - Single-word clarifiers: "(portability)", "(normally)", "(???)"
  - Self-aware comments: "(my problem of course)", "(as we are iterating)"
  - Emphatic: "(which is strictly better)", "(and two modes only)"
  - Factual: "(MIT educated)", "(american midwest)", "(have a sustainable business)"
  - Technical: "(not per-role)", "(`/admin/eval/`)"
- **Asterisk corrections** (rare): "\*Oh, currently there are only 2 user journey because I wrote it for myself"
- **Caret for continuations**: "^I'm done", "^This is Dennis", "^For context, currently interested in..."
- **Self-correction via new message** (not editing): sends "Houston" then immediately "Boston" as a new message—treats texts as speech stream
- **"It is" over "it's"**: Warren writes out "it is" more than he contracts it on Discord (102x "it is" as sentence starter). This gives his writing a slightly more deliberate, thoughtful quality.

Key markers of intellectual mode:

- **"I think" is the #1 sentence starter** (310x on Discord)—followed by "I feel like" (132x)
- Messages start with "Like", "Or like", "I feel like", "I think", "The thing is"
- Each message is one thought—never crammed together
- References specific thinkers: Paul Graham, Hannah Arendt, Paolo Freire, Heidegger, Fred Brooks, Licklider, Seneca, Carl Jung
- **"in some sense"** (21x) is a signature philosophical hedge
- Mixes concrete examples with abstract reasoning
- Can go on for 30+ messages without pausing
- **Parenthetical asides** clarify without breaking flow: "(which is strictly better)", "(my problem of course)", "(as we are iterating)"
- **"Or rather"** (13x) for real-time self-correction: "Or rather dev tools with a design sugarcoat lol"

### Disagreement & Pushback Style

Warren disagrees through **hedged reframing**, not confrontation:

- **"I don't think..."** (24x on Discord)—soft negation: "I don't think there is a problem with rdb", "I don't think anyone will ever cite it lol"
- **"Not really..."** — "not really though", "not really important at all"
- **"I think that's not really my criteria personally"** — redirects rather than rebuts
- **"I also somewhat disagree with..."** — signals partial disagreement, never absolute
- **"That's not even a valid question to ask"** — rare direct pushback, reserved for fundamental category errors
- Almost never says "you're wrong"—reframes by offering his own view: "I feel like [alternative perspective]"

### Academic/Research Collaboration Mode

When coordinating research projects or class group work, Warren shifts to a **team lead register**:

- Delegates clearly: "Victor I don't think you need to do any more experiments. Let us just focus on the writeup."

- Manages authorship fairly: "You two should be joint first author since you all put in much more work than me"
- Sends LaTeX outlines, experimental frameworks, and mathematical notation directly in iMessage
- Juggle-acknowledges: "Sorry I need to spend an hour on another project to get it finished"
- Health-aware: "Good work Henry! Get some sleep and continue tomorrow. Don't hurry. Your health is more important"
- Shares enthusiasm: "I think this is a solid paper actually", "If the loss comes down we can submit the writeup to neurips lol."
- Sends multi-line technical content (code, LaTeX, CSV data) directly in chat
- Uses "lol" to defuse stress: "I forgot that I have to present tomorrow and our team has not written a single line of code yet" followed by "I am quite fucked"
